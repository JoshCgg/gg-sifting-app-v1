Module 1 — Vision & Foundations
Why we go · Gateway cities · GG's strategy

Why Does Missions Happen?

The fact that God's name is not praised in every land among every people is an injustice against God. Missions isn't about getting a great praise report — it's about joining God on His mission to make His glory known to all people. It's not about us, our plans, our goals, our name. It's about Him.

"God is most glorified in you when you are most satisfied in Him." — John Piper

Missions Exists Because Worship Doesn't

The ultimate goal of the church is the glory of God. Missions is penultimate — worship is ultimate. World evangelization is the pathway to maximum joy and maximum worship. When the kingdom finally comes in glory, missions will cease.

Why Gateway Cities?

  • By 2030, over 75% of the world's population will live in major cities
  • Cities are influential centers — ministry here goes beyond city borders into the world
  • Being away from home, diaspora peoples are often more receptive to the Gospel
  • A person reached here can carry the Gospel back to their home country

GG's 4 Goals for Short-Term Teams

  • 🙏 Prayer — Cover our areas in prayer; battle for hearts and minds
  • 📢 Gospel Saturation — Sow the gospel broadly in a biblically and culturally appropriate way
  • 🕊️ Find Persons of Peace — Locate those ready to receive Jesus and reach their community
  • 🤝 Build Goodwill — Discern spiritual state of individuals; connect them to long-term workers
"So neither he who plants nor he who waters is anything, but only God who gives the growth." — 1 Corinthians 3:7

Module 2 — Spiritual Preparation
Prayer is the beginning, middle, and end of any meaningful trip
"Prayer is the beginning, middle, and end of any meaningful short-term trip. If you don't intend to pray, don't plan to come."

Before You Go Out Each Day

  • Spend time in personal prayer and Scripture — don't skip quiet time
  • Confess anything that might hinder the Spirit's work through you
  • Ask God to fill you afresh — not your skill, but His power
  • Pray with your team before leaving; lay hands on each person in groups of 3

What to Surrender

  • Results are God's job — your job is faithfulness
  • Many teams spend weeks planning logistics but little time preparing spiritually, then wonder why God's voice is unclear
  • Ministry among least-reached peoples requires a redefinition of what missions is about
  • A lack of spiritual boldness is a sign of not operating in the Holy Spirit's power

Build a Prayer Team at Home

Commit yourself to developing a team of prayer partners who will pray daily for you while you are here. The work depends on it.

"The harvest is plentiful but the workers are few. Ask the Lord of the harvest to send out workers into his harvest field." — Matthew 9:37–38

Module 3 — My Story & His Story
Prepare your testimony · Learn the Korban tool
"Our job is not to save people, but to expose them to the power of the Gospel so the Holy Spirit can convict them of sin and show them the way of salvation."

Telling My Story — Prepare Your Testimony

Read John 4:4–42. Notice how God used the Samaritan woman's simple testimony as a bridge to point her people to Jesus. This is our goal — introducing people to Jesus and helping them discover God's Word for themselves.

Your testimony should have one key adjective describing who you were before Jesus, and one key adjective describing what changed. Keep it to around 60 seconds. Make sure it's relevant to people who are not from a Christian background.

Your Testimony Framework

  • ✏️ My life before Jesus: (one honest adjective + brief story)
  • ✏️ How I met Jesus: (keep it simple — what happened?)
  • ✏️ How my life has changed: (one adjective + what's different now)

If you were saved very young, share a testimony of a time God intervened in your life and changed things for you.

Telling His Story — The Korban (Sacrifice) Tool

Korban is a powerful way to sift for Persons of Peace within Muslim communities. It uses the concept of sacrifice — deeply familiar in Islamic culture — as a bridge to the cross.

📺 Watch the example video before your trip: youtube.com/watch?v=eB3XwF33v3Q

"Korbani" — Sacrifice. This single word opens doors with Muslims that few other words can.

Key Bridge Words to Learn

  • 🕌 Assalamu Alaikum — Peace be upon you (Arabic/Urdu greeting)
  • 🇧🇩 Kemon achen — How are you? (Bengali)
  • 🙏 Namaste — Greeting (Hindi/Nepali)
  • ✝️ Isa Al Masih — Jesus the Messiah (use instead of "Jesus")
  • 🐑 Korbani — Sacrifice (the bridge to the cross)

Practice Assignment

Before your next team meeting, share your testimony and the Korban story with someone who is not yet a Christian. Come ready to debrief.

Module 4 — Intro to Islam & Cultural Tips
What Muslims believe · How to engage respectfully

The Five Pillars of Islam

  • Shahada — "There is no God but Allah and Muhammad is His prophet" — recited at birth and death
  • Salat — Ritual prayer 5 times daily facing Mecca (dawn, midday, mid-afternoon, sunset, two hours after sunset)
  • Sawm — Fasting during Ramadan from sunrise to sunset
  • Zakat — Giving 2.5% of accumulated wealth annually to the poor or mosque
  • Hajj — Pilgrimage to Mecca at least once in a lifetime

Key Doctrines to Understand

  • Allah is absolute and has no partners — avoid "Son of God" language initially
  • Isa (Jesus) is honored as a prophet, Messiah, Word of Allah, and Spirit of Allah — 97 references in the Qur'an
  • Muslims believe the Bible has been corrupted — use "Injil" (Gospel) language; don't argue this point
  • Destiny (Insya-Allah / "If God wills") — everything is in Allah's hands; compare James 4:13–15
  • Good deeds must outweigh bad for Paradise — they have no assurance of salvation

Bridge Points for Conversation

  • Start with Isa Al Masih — Muslims deeply respect Isa; beginning here opens hearts
  • Use Korbani — The sacrifice concept powerfully connects to the cross
  • Opening question: "What do you think about Isa Al Masih?"
  • The Qur'an affirms Isa was born of a virgin, performed miracles, and will return — build from there

Cultural Tips — Relating Well

  • Muslims value hospitality over schedules — be flexible and unhurried
  • Use "Selam" or "Assalamu Alaikum" as a greeting — they will light up
  • Remove shoes when entering a home if they do
  • Accept offered tea or food — refusing can offend deeply
  • Touching/handshakes are typically same-sex only; don't offer your hand to the opposite gender first
  • Be careful laying hands on Muslims while praying — ask permission first; some find it offensive
  • Don't press conversation during prayer times (5 daily)
  • Men primarily with men; women with women

Day 1 — Understanding Unreached People Groups
Romans 10:13-15 · What is a UPG and why should we care?
"How then will they call on him in whom they have not believed? And how are they to believe in him of whom they have never heard?" — Romans 10:14

Introduction

Many people worldwide still have no access to the gospel. There are thousands of Unreached People Groups (UPGs) — groups with less than 2% evangelical Christians and less than 5% Christian adherents — with no resources to spread the gospel among their own people.

1. What is an Unreached People Group?

Paul declares in Romans 10 that "everyone who calls on the name of the Lord will be saved" — then immediately raises the challenge with four questions about access. Over 3.2 billion people belong to unreached groups (more than 40% of the world's population). There are over 7,000 UPGs in the world, including 180 living right here in North America. The difference between the lost and the unreached is access — UPGs often have no churches, Bibles, or Christians who speak their language.

2. Why Do UPGs Still Exist?

Paul recounts in 2 Corinthians 11 the trials he faced bringing the gospel. Five key barriers remain today:

  • Geographic — Some places can only be reached by plane, train, boat, horseback, then a hike
  • Cultural & Religious — Many live in countries where conversion is discouraged or forbidden
  • Political — Government restrictions prevent evangelism and persecute Christians
  • Priority — Only 1.7% of Christian giving goes to the unreached; only 3% of missionaries go to them
  • Awareness — Many Christians simply don't know how many groups have little access to the Gospel

3. Why Should We Care?

  • Matthew 28:19 — Jesus commands disciples of ALL nations, not just around our church
  • Revelation 7:9 — God's plan is for people from every nation, tribe, and language to worship Jesus
  • Romans 10:14-15 — The unreached will only hear about Jesus if someone goes to them

Questions to Consider

  • What is keeping people from calling upon the Lord and being saved, according to Paul?
  • What do you need to change in your prayers, giving, and obedience to be more faithful to reaching the unreached?

Day 2 — Biblical Mandate to Reach UPGs
Genesis 12 · Matthew 28 · Revelation 7 · God's global mission
"I will bless you and make your name great, so that you will be a blessing... and in you all the families of the earth shall be blessed." — Genesis 12:2-3

Introduction

From the Old Testament to the New Testament, God has given those who believe in Him the mission to reach the unreached. This mission is not new — it has been His plan from the beginning.

1. The Global Vision of the Old Testament

In Genesis 12:1-3, God calls Abram with four promises: a land (Place), a great nation (People), blessing him to be a blessing (Platform), and blessing all peoples of the world through him (Purpose). Psalm 67 echoes this — God's blessing on His people has a purpose: that His saving power be known among all nations. Isaiah 49 continues this thread: God makes His people a light for the nations so that His salvation may reach to the end of the earth.

2. The Global Mandate of the New Testament

Jesus gives a global mandate in every Gospel and in Acts:

  • Matthew 28:18-20 — "Go and make disciples" of all nations
  • Mark 16:15 — "Proclaim the Gospel" to the whole creation
  • Luke 24:47 — Repentance proclaimed to all nations
  • John 20:21 — "As the Father has sent me, even so I am sending you"
  • Acts 1:8 — Witnesses "to the end of the earth"

3. The Global Gathering of Heaven

Revelation 5:9, 7:9, and 22:2 show the culmination of God's mission: a multitude from every tribe and language and people and nation worshipping around the throne. This vision should create urgency and assurance — our work is not in vain.

Questions to Consider

  • What is the purpose of God blessing His people in Genesis, Psalms, and Isaiah?
  • Who and where does Jesus expect the mission of God to go?
  • How does my current involvement in missions contribute to the global gathering of heaven?

Day 3 — God's Sovereignty, Our Obedience
Acts 17 · God has brought the nations here
"He made from one man every nation of mankind to live on all the face of the earth, having determined allotted periods and the boundaries of their dwelling place." — Acts 17:26

Introduction

In Acts 17, Paul walked into Athens and saw what God had sovereignly arranged. The same opportunity exists in North America's global cities today. In God's sovereignty, people from all over the world — many unreached with the Gospel — have come here. This is not random. This is an open door.

1. God's Sovereignty in the Movement of People

Paul used the altar "to the unknown god" as a bridge to make the Sovereign God known. He declared that God determines when and where nations are established and where people groups find their dwelling place. Nothing about this moment is random or accidental — including the fact that you are here this week.

2. The Opportunity in Global Gateway Cities

When unreached people groups move to North American cities, an incredible opportunity opens. The barriers that once prevented access are removed:

  • More than 4 million people from UPGs live in the New York Metropolitan Area
  • Houston is home to 10 significantly unreached people groups from Central Asia, the Middle East, and South Asia
  • Over 170 nationalities live in the greater Washington D.C. area
  • By 2050, 68% of the world's population will live in urban areas

3. A Great Commission Response

When people here are reached with the Gospel, that Gospel finds its way back to their country of birth through ongoing communication. Reaching them here has a multiplying effect in some of the hardest-to-reach places on earth.

Questions to Consider

  • Who ultimately determines the movement of people groups throughout time, according to Paul?
  • What barriers to reaching UPGs has God's sovereignty already removed by bringing people here?
  • How does this reality shape a Great Commission response from you and your church?

Day 4 — The Church Sent
Luke 10 · The harvest is plentiful, the laborers are few
"The harvest is plentiful, but the laborers are few. Therefore pray earnestly to the Lord of the harvest to send out laborers into his harvest." — Luke 10:2

Introduction

We now know: over 7,000 UPGs in the world. Over 180 in North America. Most in urban centers. The question is no longer informational — it is personal. What will you do?

1. A Biblical Response — The Harvest Is Plentiful

In Luke 10, Jesus appoints and sends a group of believers on His mission — into what appears to be an extremely difficult field. His words before sending them: "The harvest is plentiful, but the laborers are few." Only 1.7% of Christian giving goes to the unreached. Only 3% of missionaries go to them. Yet there is growing openness as unreached people come to North American cities. The harvest is still plentiful. The laborers are still few. You are here this week as one response to that.

2. Discover, Pray, and Reach Your Mission Field

  • Discover — The Global Gates research team can help identify what UPGs are in your area
  • Pray — Begin praying specifically for the people groups near you and your church
  • Reach — Global Gates missionary catalysts can provide training, resources, and coaching
  • Commit — Consider another Sifting Week, a Gap Year, internship, or long-term mission pathway

3. Your Next Step

Take time to reflect on this week — your encounters, your quiet times, your prayer walks. What has God shown you that you need to carry home? Write it down. Share it with someone. Act on it.

Questions to Consider

  • What is "plentiful" and what is "few" according to Jesus in Luke 10:2?
  • What are Jesus' two responses to the reality of an abundant harvest and few laborers?
  • What is one concrete next step God is calling you to take after this week?

Prayer Walking Guide
Praying on-site with insight for your specific area
"Prayer walking is praying on-site with insight for specific areas around you, and covering these areas with God's presence as you walk."

Why Walk While You Pray?

  • Walking physically integrates you in a place — you begin to pray with your whole body, not just your mind
  • Being there makes you aware of specific things to pray for that you'd never think of at home
  • Walking is an act of confidence in God's power over a situation
  • Your presence itself is a witness — people notice that someone cares enough to show up

Before You Go Out

  • Begin with worship — dedicate yourself and the next few hours to God
  • Read a passage to focus your attention on what you're about to do
  • Be quiet before God and listen — tune yourself to His voice
  • Resolve any team tensions before you start — effective prayer is compromised by team conflict

Organizing the Walk

  • Divide into groups of 2–3 — enough for agreement, not so many that some can't participate
  • Split up experienced prayer walkers between groups
  • Decide on a route; be flexible with timing — 1–2 hours is a good start
  • Balance three elements: worship · spiritual warfare · welcoming Christ

What to Pray

  • Spiritual breakthrough in every home on every street
  • Salvations — pray for people by name when you know them
  • For Persons of Peace — ask God to highlight them to you
  • Against spiritual darkness — that bondage be broken
  • For laborers — more long-term workers for this harvest

If Asked What You're Doing

"We're praying blessings over the neighborhood — can we pray for you?"

Note where prayer feels heavy or blocked — report it to your team leader. Take breaks — this is spiritually exhausting work.

Holy Spirit's Power — John 14:12
Why you can do greater things than you think
"Whoever believes in me will also do the works that I do; and greater works than these will he do, because I am going to the Father." — John 14:12

Knowledge vs. Power

The apostles had 3½ years with Jesus — knowledge of Him — but at the crucifixion they all deserted Him. They had knowledge but not His power. Something had to change.

In Acts 1, God sent the Holy Spirit. When Jesus went to heaven, God put His Spirit inside every believer. We can now be "little Christs" — carrying His presence, doing His works.

Peter Before and After Pentecost

In the Garden of Gethsemane, Peter denied Christ three times. A few months later, that same Peter stood and boldly proclaimed Jesus to thousands. Was the situation less dangerous? No. The Spirit had come.

"A lack of spiritual boldness is a sign that we are not operating in the power of the Holy Spirit."

Be Filled Daily

This isn't a one-time experience. You need to be filled with the Holy Spirit every day. Without Him, you will quickly get discouraged and stop serving.

"Do not be drunk with wine... but be filled with the Spirit." — Ephesians 5:18

Pre-Outreach Prayer

Break into groups of 3. Take turns laying hands on each person. Ask God to fill them so that when they speak today, their words carry the very authority of God.

3 Circles Gospel Presentation
Visual gospel tool that crosses language barriers

How to Begin

"Can I share with you a picture of how God met my greatest need?" Draw as you talk. No paper? Draw in the air or on your hand.

Circle 1 — God's Perfect Design

Draw a circle. God created everything good — us, relationships, the world. This is where we were made to live.

Circle 2 — Sin → Brokenness

Draw an arrow out, then a jagged broken circle. Everywhere we look we see brokenness. We try to escape it on our own — but we can't get back.

The Cross — Jesus (Isa Al Masih / Korbani)

  • Jesus (God's Spirit and God's Word) came to earth — perfect life, no sin
  • Died as Korbani — sacrifice — to pay for the sin and brokenness of the world
  • Rose 3 days later and returned to heaven
"If we TURN from sin and BELIEVE in Jesus, through Him we are restored to God's Perfect Design." — Romans 10:9

The Three Responses

  • 🟢 Green — Believed: Pray together immediately. Begin 411 discipleship. Call team leader.
  • 🟡 Yellow — Open: Schedule follow-up. Give Jesus film. Note their address and contact info.
  • 🔴 Red — Not ready: Offer prayer. Leave film. Keep relationship warm. Move on and keep searching.

You will offer a Jesus film to everyone you meet as a parting gift.

Korban Quick Reference
Field card for the sacrifice bridge — use with Muslim neighbors
"Korbani" — Sacrifice. This concept is deeply embedded in Islamic culture and powerfully bridges to the cross.

The Bridge in One Sentence

Muslims understand and deeply value sacrifice (Korbani). Eid al-Adha — the Festival of Sacrifice — commemorates Ibrahim's willingness to sacrifice his son. Use this shared concept to introduce Jesus as God's ultimate Korbani.

Opening the Conversation

  • "What do you think about Isa Al Masih?"
  • "Have you heard of Korbani — the idea of a perfect sacrifice for sin?"
  • "Can I show you something about Isa from the Injil (Gospel)?"

Key Points to Cover

  • God requires a sacrifice for sin — this is in the Taurat (Torah) and Injil (Gospel)
  • Every animal sacrifice pointed forward to the one perfect Korbani
  • Isa Al Masih is that Korbani — sinless, chosen by God, raised from death
  • Through Isa's Korbani, we can be forgiven and restored to God
"Behold, the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world!" — John 1:29

House of Peace Search — Luke 10
Finding Persons of Peace door-to-door
"Whatever house you enter, first say 'Peace be to this house!'" — Luke 10:5

Suggested Script

  • Introduce: "Hi, I'm _______ and this is my friend _______"
  • Care: "...and we're out serving our community by asking God to bless this neighborhood and provide for people's needs."
  • Ask: "Is there anything we can pray for you and your family?"
  • Listen. Then respond based on their answer.

If YES — Pray, Then Transition

"God, thank you that you love [name] and care for their needs. We pray in Jesus' name that you would bless this family by revealing yourself to them in a very special way..." Then: "Thank you for letting us pray for you. I'm curious — are you religious?" → Continue to Gospel.

If NO — Gently Transition

"Ok. Are you sure? Are you not religious at all?" → Try to move to Gospel dialogue. If they close the door, thank them and move on.

Three Signs of a Person of Peace

  • ✅ Accepts the Messenger — welcomes you warmly
  • ✅ Accepts the Message — receptive to what you share
  • ✅ Accepts the Mission — wants others in their community to know

When You Find One

Call your team leader immediately. Stay and invest — the Person of Peace gathers their own community. Don't move on too fast. Schedule a Bible study. Bring a local believer if possible.

3 P's When You Enter a Home

  • ⚡ Power — Pray in the authority of Jesus
  • ☮️ Presence — Embody the peace of Christ
  • 📢 Proclamation — Speak the Good News

411 Discipleship Tool
4 questions · 1 paper · 1 hour — for new disciples
"Go therefore and make disciples of all nations..." — Matthew 28:19

The 4 Questions

  • ❓ WHY? Your identity as Ambassador — making disciples isn't just what you do, it's who you are (2 Cor. 5:17–21)
  • 👤 WHO? Persons of Peace + your Oikos (household network)
  • 💬 WHAT? The 3 Circles gospel presentation
  • 📅 WHEN? Specific calendar goals before they leave

Oikos Map (WHO? Part 2)

Draw a circle with the person's name in the center. Map relationships far from God — go out 4 generations. This becomes their personal prayer and evangelism strategy. Pray for these people by name right now.

4 WHEN? Goals to Set Before They Leave

  • 📆 PRAY — Daily time to pray for their Oikos (set a specific time)
  • 👋 SHARE — One person from their Oikos to share the gospel with this week (name + day/time)
  • 🎓 TRAIN — When will they train someone else in 411?
  • 🚀 START — First Commands of Christ meeting at obeychrist.com (When? Where? Who else?)

If Someone Believes — Do This Immediately

  • Pray together right then
  • Begin 411 on the spot — don't wait
  • Schedule your next meeting before you leave them
  • Call your team leader
